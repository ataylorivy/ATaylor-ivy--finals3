Overview:

Uromastyx is a genus of lizards which usually reside in northern africa aswell as parts of the middle east. The most profound feature of this species of lizard are their spiney tails, this tail is commonly used to hit predators, and protect the rest of their body when under cover.

What do they eat?

Uromastyx's are a herbivorous lizard, this means they eat plants.
Here is a small list of plants and seeds they enjoy include:

  • kale
  • romaine lettuce
  • Mustard greens
  • Endive
  • Dandelion greens
  • Lentils
  • Millets

Their diet should be 80-90% leafy greens (usually sprinkled with seeds), and 5-10% fruits only as a treat.
make sure to dust their food with calcium and vitamin D3 supplements to prevent deficiencies.

Lighting, basking, air temp, and UV-B information:

  • Basking temperature: 120F (49C)
  • Hot-Side air temp: 90F (32C)
  • Cold-Side air temp: 75F (24C)
  • UV-B strength: 14%, ALWAYS use a long light, and
    NEVER use a coil light as they do not provide proper UV-B

Reccomendations for uromastyx's enclosure:

  • DO NOT PUT ANY LOOSE ROCKS ON TOP OF THEIR SUBSTRATE AS THEY ARE A BURROWING SPECIES,
    AND IF THEY BURROW UNDER AND THE ROCKS COLLAPSE IT CAN KILL THEM!
  • use a substrate which contains a mixture of sand and soil
    (though if your not getting sand from a reptile store use play sand)
  • NEVER USE SEEDS AS A SUBSTRATE AS IT IS A MISCONCEPTION AND ONLY STRESSES THEM OUT
  • make lots of places for them to hide away in, such as mini "caves" or logs
  • branches, or other kinds of places to climb on (such as a rocky enclosure background)
  • Humidity: 10-30% (IMPORTANT)

Misc info:

  • Price: $100-1200, HEAVILY depends on what species of uromastyx
  • Enclosure size: 4 foot length * 2 foot width * 2 foot height (depends on species)
  • lifespan: 15 to 20 years, though some have been recorded to be 30 years old.
  • Behavior: shy, unlikley to be out alot at first (can differ for each individual)

Fun facts:

The largest uromastyx species is the egyptian uromastyx, with many getting to over 30 inches in length

The oldest uromastyx ever was a uromastyx nigriventris, (moroccan uromastyx) which lived for at least 46 years
