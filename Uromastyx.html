What do they eat?

Uromastyx's are a herbivorous lizard, this means they eat plants.
Here is a small list of plants and seeds they enjoy include:

  1. kale
  2. romaine lettuce
  3. Mustard greens
  4. Endive
  5. Dandelion greens
  6. Lentils
  7. Millets

Overview:

Uromastyx is a genus of lizards which usually reside in northern africa aswell as parts of the middle east. The most profound feature of this type of lizard is their spiney/spikey tail, this tail is commonly used to hit predators, and protect the rest of their body when under cover.

Lighting, basking, and UV-B information:

  • Temperature: 120 F (49 C)
  • UV-B strength: 14%, ALWAYS use a long light, and NEVER use a coil light

Reccomendations for enclosures:

  • DO NOT PUT ANY LOOSE ROCKS ON TOP OF THEIR SUBSTRATE AS THEY ARE A BURROWING SPECIES,
    AND IF THEY BURROW UNDER AND THE ROCKS COLLAPSE IT CAN KILL THEM!
  • substrate which contains a mixture of sand,
  • lots of places for them to hide away in, such as mini "caves" or logs
  • branches, or other kinds of places to climb on (such as a rocky enclosure background)

Misc info:

  • Price: $100-1200, HEAVILY depends on what species of uromastyx
  • Enclosure size: 4 foot length * 2 foot width * 2 foot height (depends on species)
  • Behavior: shy, unlikley to be out alot at first (can differ for each individual)
